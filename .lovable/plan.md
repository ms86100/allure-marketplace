

# Landing Page Repositioning: From "Marketplace" to "What No App Can Deliver"

## The Problem

Every section of the current landing page positions Sociva as a marketplace competing with Blinkit/Swiggy. The hero says "Shop From Neighbors You Trust" -- this invites direct comparison with delivery apps where Sociva loses on speed. The pain points frame generic delivery problems. The features list reads like any e-commerce platform.

Sociva's actual competitive advantage is **things that don't exist on any delivery app**: your neighbor's homemade thepla, the retired uncle who tutors math, the aunt who makes birthday cakes to order, the plumber who lives in B-block. No algorithm, no dark store, no 10-minute promise can replicate this.

## What Changes

Pure copy and content rewrite across 6 landing components. No structural, layout, or animation changes. Same components, same sections, same design system -- different story.

### 1. LandingHero -- New headline and framing

**Current:** "Tired of Strangers? Shop From Neighbors You Trust."
**New:** "What Your Neighbor Makes, No App Can Deliver."

Sub-copy shifts from "stop relying on delivery apps" (defensive) to celebrating what's uniquely available here (offensive). Badge changes from "Free for Buyers" to "Only from verified neighbors."

### 2. LandingPainPoints -- Reframe around exclusivity, not generic delivery problems

**Current cards:**
- "Ordering from unknown sellers?" (generic trust)
- "Paying delivery fees?" (price complaint)
- "Neighbor has no platform?" (seller-side)

**New cards -- frame what users are MISSING:**
- "Mrs. Sharma's dal makhani is legendary. But she's not on Zomato." → Only here can you order from home kitchens in your building
- "You've called 5 plumbers from Google. None showed up." → Verified residents who live 2 minutes away
- "Your kid needs math help. The retired professor lives in C-block." → Services from neighbors you already see daily

### 3. LandingFeatures -- Lead with what's impossible elsewhere

Reorder and rewrite the 6 benefit cards to emphasize **exclusivity** over generic marketplace features:
- "Home Kitchens, Not Cloud Kitchens" (food you can't get anywhere else)
- "Services From People You See Every Day" (accountability through proximity)
- "Know the Maker, Not Just the Rating" (transparency impossible at scale)
- "Custom Orders, Just Ask" (chat-based customization unique to neighbor commerce)
- "No Surge Pricing, No Algorithms" (human-to-human, not platform-mediated)
- "Reviews From People You'll Meet in the Elevator" (unfakeable social proof)

### 4. LandingTestimonials -- Stories about irreplaceability

Replace generic marketplace testimonials with stories that emphasize "I can't get this anywhere else":
- A parent ordering specific dietary meals for their child from a neighbor who understands the allergy
- A seller who tried Zomato/Swiggy but found the commissions killed their margins -- here they keep everything
- A resident who found their building's best-kept-secret service provider

### 5. LandingFinalCTA -- Emotional, not transactional

**Current:** "Your Community Is Waiting."
**New:** "The Best Things in Your Society Aren't on Any App. Until Now."

### 6. LandingPricing -- Seller positioning shift

Seller card description changes from generic "list and sell" to: "Your neighbor's kitchen, craft studio, or skill -- finally has a storefront. Zero commission. Zero listing fees."
